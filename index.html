The Challenges

To redesign the Black Lives Matter Canada webpage to provide BLM Canada with a more credible website, generating more traffic and donations. Organizing the information will also help in educating the masses on the systemic racism within our society.

My part in the process was the facilite and aid in the research and information architecture. Such task as:

  • User Testing
  • Card Sorting
  • Sitemap Crafting

Research

In this redesign project, our group planned and conducted a google survey and 4 interviews with current users and potential users via zoom

BLM- Canada is an organization that receives a lot of traffic, so we wanted to see how people reacted to the site itself. The main reason for the usability testing was to see how the organization of the site could be improved and what the pain point were when navigating through the site. We asked user to complete three tasks while navigating the website:

  • Make a donation in honour of an individual and then return to the homepage
  • Find if a specific demand has been met or not and
  • Find the search bar and use it

Based off of our user research and findings we were able to create our user person.

Definition & Ideation

We then created a sitemap for our site. After completing our card sorting sessions, we made some improvements to the sitemap. Some of the improvements involved the reorganization of some subcategories and addition to the utilities of the site.

As we continued to and using data from information architecture work, we were able to make further iteration on the wireframes from which we could complete user testing from.

Definition & Ideation

Purpose: To inform design decisions and identify usability issues by revealing areas of confusion and uncover opportunities to improve the overall user experience.

Testing Plan: We first conducted a preference test to see which version of the site users liked more.

Next we conducted 4 user testing and had them complete 3 tasks: Donate $25 in honour of someone, find information related to carding, and locating their social media links.
